François Truffaut

Icon of the French New Wave movement

François Roland Truffaut

Actor. Film critic. Film maker. Producer. Screenwriter

He is one of the founders of the French New Wave and had a career lasting over a quarter of a century. He worked on more than 25 films and remains one of the most appreciated director from the French film industry. In his career, he was also a screenwriter, producer and actor. We have selected some of his films that we are reccomending from his vast work and this week we are covering Jim and Jules in our filmography section.

The Film Buffs Top 3 Movies

Jules and Jim

(1962)

Jules and Jim is a French New Wave romantic drama film, directed, produced and written by François Truffaut. The film is based on Henri-Pierre Roché's 1953 semi-autobiographical novel that describes his relationship with young writer Franz Hessel and Helen Grund. Truffaut discovered the book in the '50s while browsing through some books at a library, in Paris.

The 400 Blows

(1959)

This is a highly personal and sensitive story that François Truffaut told from the point of view of his cinematic counterpart, Antoine Doinel (Jean-Pierre Léaud). The 400 Blows (Les quatre cents coups) is a reflextion of Truffaut’s own childhood and traumas, from having issues with his family, the teachers who were oppresive, and petty crime. Apart from being a masterpiece, this film made Truffaut the auteur of the French New Wave.

Shoot the Piano Player

(1960)

This is one of François Truffaut's most playful films. Part thriller, part comedy, part tragedy, the film presents the adventures of piano player Charlie (Charles Aznavour) as he discoveres the criminal underworld and gets involved in a whirlwind love affair. It's considered an homage to the American gangster film and is loaded with guns, puns, clowns and thugs.
